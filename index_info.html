WOLRD USAGE OF ELECTRICITY FROM FOSSIL FUEL AND CARBON DIOXIDE EMISSIONS AS A RESULT OF ELECTRICY CONSUMPTION

Below are two visualisations which show the global usage of electricity sourced from fossil fuels and the relationship between GDP and tonnes of carbon dioxide emissions as a result of electricity consumption.

Fossil Fuels

Fossil fuels include coal, petroleum, natural gas, oil shales, bitumens, tar sands and heavy oils. Fossil fuel power plants burn fossil fuel materials in order to create heat which is in turns used to generate steam which drives turbines generating electricity.

Carbon Dioxide Emissions and GDP

Is there a relationship between Carbon Dioxide Emissions from electricity consumption and the GDP of a country? Use the interactive Continent and Population Selectors to view how this relationship trends for different regions of the world and different populations.

This visualisation is created by Elena Doncila using resources from Kadek Satriadi. The datasource is some data repository online.
